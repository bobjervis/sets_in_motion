Animations

The sub-window that you can use to view an animation of a sequence is the Animations view.  It appears in the right-hand pane of the main window.

The image at right shows an example of the Animations view.

Dancers appear as blue squares (for boys) and pink circles (for girls).  The number written in these figures is the couple nummber for the dancer.  A small black semi-circle is drawn to indicate the location of the dancer's nose.

As you resize the window, the size of the figures will change to fit the image to the available space.

Across the top of the view, you will see a row of controls.

On the far left as four buttons.  They are marked using standard playback symbols.  From left to right, they are 'rewind to the beginning', rewind one 'call', play/pause and forward one call.

Note that the image on the play/pause button will change from when paused to when playing.  The visible symbol declares what pressing the button should do.

To the right of the control buttons is a track with a circular green spot.  This is a time scale.  The location of the green spot marks the relative time within the total sequence that the image currently shows.  You will see that this spot moves across the track as the animation plays.

Note that pressing the rewind, back one call or forward one call buttons while the animation is playing does not cause it to pause.  Thus, you can actively 'loop' the animation by repeatedly playing the back or rewind buttons.

To the right of the time track is a set of radio-buttons.  Click on one of the circles to select the speed you want to play the animation.  The program will remember the speed setting.

Slowest speed is about half that of Normal.  Fastest speed is about twice that of Normal and the other speeds are intermediate between the extremes.  Note that Normal speed is currently set to about 120 beats per minute.
