Call Analysis Browser

The sub-window that you can use to drill into the details of a call is called the Call Analysis Browser.  It will appear in the right-hand pane of the main window whenever you click on a drill-down button in a Sequence Editor.

The image at right shows a sample sequence in which the drilldown button of the second call, "circle to a line", has been clicked.

In the right-hand pane, you see Back and Forward  buttons at the top of the window.  When you have drilled into several pages, you can use these buttons to navigate back and forward through the pages.  (Note that these buttons do not currently change to indicate whether there are more pages available if you were to click on them.)

In the page view itself, you see the structured break down of the call itself at the top of the page.  Immediately below that is a diagram of the dancer's starting formation.  Depending on what was needed to perform the call, what appears below varies.

In this case, the call had to be performed by breaking the square down into pieces and performing the call in each piece.  You see to facing couples drawn from the starting formation, then under each group the call as applied to that group.  Note that unless the call is a primitive, when the call is broken down into subgroups like this, you will be offered a drilldown button to look in detail at each group's call.

When a call is broken down into sub-groups like this, the final dancer positions may not be exactly right.  In such a case, the program must 'breathe' the formation to arrive at the final dancer configuration.  If that was needed, then a 'Before Breathing' box as drawn (as in this case) with the un-adjusted positions of the dancers.

In this case, the adjustment was to slide the two lines closer together.

A multi-step call will show a series of sub-calls and diagrams as in the example on the left.  In this case we see a part of the drill-down for "shoot the star to another thar".

There are technical reasons why the individual primitive calls appear twice each, but in future releases the analysis pages will get cleaned up so the output is hopefully a little less cluttered and easier to comprehend.
