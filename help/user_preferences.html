Preferences

The sub-window that allows you to view and modify a variety of program parameters is called the Preferences view.  It will appear in the right-hand pane of the main window.

The image above shows the appearance of the preferences view. 

Any value that you change either in the Preferences view or wherever else it appears in the program will be stored immediately to disk.  You never have to explicitly save preference values, nor is there any notion of undoing them.  If you are ever unsure of their current setting, a quick look at the Preferences view is designed to let you know what their values currently.

The Animation speed is the same set of speed buttons that appears in the Animator.  The selected speed varies where Slowest is about half the speed of Normal speed and Fastest is about twice as fast as Normal.  The other values are in between.

The two Allow editing' check boxes correspond to locking or unlocking the respective Definitions files.

The various font settings control how the text of sequences appear either in the Performance Window or when printed on paper.  Currently there is no way to control the fonts or text size in the main window.

For each font, you may choose the font family from a menu, but if you have too many fonts, the list will extend past the bottom of your screen and there is no way to scroll it currently.

A sample of each font is printed to the right, using the various settings as you have entered them.

Finally in the very bottom of the Preferences pane is a distorted mockup of a piece of paper.  The upper grey area is the header space of the page and the lower grey area is the body of the sequence on a page.  The various margins identified govern how a sequence is positioned relative to the edges of the paper or in the case of the header margin, the edges of the respective text areas on the page.  Note that the units default to inches, but that field is selectable.

Use the Windows printer dialog to adjust settings such as paper size for your printer.  Margins are computed based on the selected paper size.

Currently, there are no preference settings for text color.
