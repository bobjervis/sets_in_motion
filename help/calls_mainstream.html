Callerlab Basic Level Calls

These calls are organized by the Callerlab call names and numbering, using the Callerlab Basic and Mainstream Definitions document dated November 1, 2009.

Sections are included below for each call, even if there is nothing said in that section.  If nothing is said, then the call should work as you would expect.

52. Cloverleaf

52.a Cloverleaf (Everyone Active)

52.b Cloverleaf (2 Couples Only Active)

This variation is implemented as well as I can understand both the definition as written and the way callers and dancers expet this to be done.  For example, the definition states that the inactive dancers move forward into the middle if they are facing that way.  However, my experience is that the inactive dancers are expected to move together to the middle spots no matter how the are facing.  The program moves inactives together regardless of facing.

62. Fold / Cross Fold

This call explicitly states that dancers should not adjust after folding.  As in many cases in square dancing, there are adjustments and there are adjustments.  It seems to be common to assume that dancers will adjust to boxes after a fold or cross fold call.  The SD program, for example, will adjust to boxes (and thereby make a column)after the call "ends fold" from parallel ocean waves.  If one had called "ends press ahead" from parallel ocean waves, the in-facing end dancers step forward to the next wave.  However, in a fold the folding dancers do not interact with the other wave.  Instead, the ends fold to face the back of the center wave dancers and the two waves spread apart to make a Z formation.  This is what Sets in Motion does.

Similarly, the call "centers cross fold" from out-facing lines, which is explicitly allowed in the definition, should produce two single-file columns with two phantom spots between each column.  Sets in Motion does this.

Sadly, Mainstream calls do not give you much ability to work with either Z's or single-file columns with phantoms.  This is likely the reason that Mainstream dancers do adjust to boxes after folds and cross folds.

To give you the option of adjusting, Sets in Motion includes the directional call "adjust to a box" (see under Directional Calls).

63. Dixie Style to an Ocean Wave

The definition as written allows this call from facing Tandems.  This would suggest that the call "dixie style to a wave" from a beginning double pass thru would have the dancers work single-file (since those are facing tandems).  This is not generally done.  Instead, a caller is expected to say something like "working single-file, dixie style to a wave".  At present, Sets in Motion does not support any phrasing like "single-file", but it does allow you to call "dixie style to a wave" from a beginning double pass thru formation.
